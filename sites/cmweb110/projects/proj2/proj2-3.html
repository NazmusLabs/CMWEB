Question

The internet is a useful tool when researching problems with coding or with the computer. It is good to get into the habit of searching for answers to coding/programming/computer issues. To that end, tell me how to set the position of a background image on my web page using css and give me a couple of different sites that explain it.

Answer

Use the following CSS property:
background-position: 100px 5px;

Source

Question

What was the average wheat yield per acre (in bushels) in the 1800's? What is it now? (you may need to find two separate websites to answer this one).

Answer

In the 1890s, the average weat yield per acre was 25 bushels. Today in Kansas, the yield is 11-70 bushels per acre.

Source

Search Site Used to Locate Answer: For information on 1800s: How low are wheat prices? Take a look back at the 1800s, 1900s, and you‚Äôll see it‚Äôs an all-time low - The Hutchinson News

For information on today: WHEAT TOUR DAY 2: 42 BUSHELS PER ACRE - SuccessfulFarming

Search Site Used to Locate Answer: Bing

Search Term Used: Search query 1: What was the average wheat yield per acre (in bushels) in the 1800's

Search query 2: What was the average wheat yield per acre (in bushels) today.

Rationale as to whether this is a reasonable answer: The answer to the first part of the question was provided by the The Hutchinson News. This is an established daily newspaper publisher for Hutchinson, Kansas founded in 1872. With over a hudnred years of operation and the fact that the public trusts them for their daily news meets my credibility criteria for information source.

As for the second part of the answer (the yield today), I referred to a back up source: Spring Wheat: Yield per Harvested Acre by County - Charts and Maps - United States Department of Agriculture
This is an official US government website, namely the US Department of Agriculture. There is no question in their credibility here. In this web page, I referred to a US map that charts the amount of weat is yeild (range from lowest to highest) with states color coded to designate yield output. From this chart, i can see that the majority of the agriculture states are producing 30-70 bushels per acre.
